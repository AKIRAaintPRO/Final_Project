Lock It Down: Why Strong Passwords Matter

Weak or reused passwords substantially increase the risk of account compromise. Use long, memorable passphrases formed from unrelated words supplemented by digits or symbols, and avoid reusing credentials across services. A reputable password manager can securely generate and store unique passwords. Additionally, enable Two‑Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever available — authenticator apps or hardware security keys provide an additional verification step that greatly reduces the risk of unauthorized access.

What you will learn

Security Risk Assessment: Why weak and reused passwords expose users to significant risk.

Password Construction: Methods for constructing strong, memorable passwords.
